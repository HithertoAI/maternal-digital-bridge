Inclusion & Rural Accessibility Strategy
This project rejects the "exclusion by design" model. To achieve SDG 1 (No Poverty) and SDG 3 (Good Health), the infrastructure must reach the last mile.

1. The Hardware Necessity
The "Human Router" Problem: Manual, paper-based verification in rural Ethiopia creates "dead zones" where birth notifications never reach the civil registry.

Mandatory Device Provisioning: The "Digital Kit" is a non-negotiable architectural component. It provides the mother with a secure touchpoint for her Level 1 Digital Wallet and Messaging/Notification triggers.

2. Connectivity & Offline Readiness
2G/3G Optimization: The system is designed for high-latency environments, using USSD-capable triggers for registration and payment notifications.

Asynchronous Sync: Rural clinics utilize a "Batch & Sync" pattern, allowing health workers to register pregnancies offline, with data syncing to the Information Mediator once a connection is established.

3. Biometric & ID Safeguards
Exception Handling: Recognizing that biometric capture (fingerprints) can fail due to manual labor or degradation, the system includes an "Assisted Enrollment" workflow.

Fayda ID Integration: Leverages the MOSIP-based national ID to ensure that even without physical documents, a mother’s identity can be verified through the Identification Pattern.
