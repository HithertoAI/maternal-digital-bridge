# Governance & The Circular Data Economy

This architecture implements a self-sustaining model for Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI) by treating data as a high-value, protected asset.

## 1. The Bauta Valve (Privacy Gatekeeper)
* **The Concept**: A technical "valve" that allows for the capture of human intent and clinical data while strictly filtering out Personally Identifiable Information (PII).
* **Function**: It acts as a privacy-preserving layer within the Information Mediator building block, ensuring data stewardship remains with the mother.
* [cite_start]**Compliance**: Aligned with the Ethiopian PDPP 2024 and the UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI.

## 2. Circular Economy Funding Model
* **Data Monetization**: Anonymized, high-quality maternal health datasets are licensed to research institutions and AI developers.
* **Closed-Loop Subsidy**: Revenue generated from data licensing is funneled back into the system to pay for the hardware kits (mobile devices) provided to rural mothers.
* **Sustainability**: This removes the reliance on perpetual government grants or donor funding, making the Maternal Digital Bridge a self-funding infrastructure.

## 3. The Agentic State Integration
* [cite_start]**Public Agents**: Utilizing AI-based "Public Agents" to assist mothers in navigating their journey, ensuring that "Night-Seeing" and "Night-Hearing" capabilities are available 24/7.
* [cite_start]**AI Blocks**: The system uses modular AI blocks to handle biometric exception handling and automated eligibility verification[cite: 153].
